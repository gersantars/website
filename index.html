Geraldine Santillana Armas

(Lima, 1998)

Peruvian artist with experience in research, cultural management, and artistic-scientific practices. Her work focuses on research-based artistic practices, art and science methodologies, non-human narratives and their possibilities. She has worked as a research assistant, co-directed a self-managed cultural space, and has been part of research and cultural production teams in Peru.

I approach technology and non-living entities, materials often perceived as inert, as political beings that pass through our bodies and shape shared realities. My work focuses on recognizing these often invisible beings as vessels of time and materialities to unfold their own stories where technology leaves traces and subtle forms of power. They carry histories and stories of extraction, violence, and desire which working with it requires embracing exposure, contagion and uncertainty as part of the process.

My practice grows from a scientific curiosity through hybrid methodologies such as DIY methods and material experimentation. Through projects rooted in specific territories and/or beings, fiction becomes a tool to imagine possibilities, premonitions, and forms of empathy across species and systems. Perhaps by paying attention to matter, we can find ways to rematerialize and move through the porous boundaries that once separated us.
