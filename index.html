Geraldine Santillana Armas

Lima, 1998

Geraldine Santillana Armas (Lima, 1998) is a visual artist with experience in research, cultural management, and interdisciplinary artistic practices. She holds a Bachelor of Fine Arts, Major in Painting from the Pontifical Catholic University of Peru (PUCP) and is currently pursuing an MFA in Ecology Futures at AKV|St. Joost (NL). Her work focuses on research-based artistic practices, art and science methodologies, non-human narratives and their possibilities. She has worked as a research assistant, co-directed a self-managed cultural space, and has been part of research and cultural production teams in Peru.

The world of orchids becomes a case study to discuss issues such as conservation, beauty and anthropocentrism.
